What is Linux?

Linux is similar to other operating systems you may have used before, including Windows, macOS or iOS (Android is based on a modified Linux kernel). Like other operating systems, Linux has a graphical interface, and the same types of software you can find on all your other devices. In many cases, a software creator may have made a Linux version of the same program you use on other operating systems. Linux was created by Linus Torvalds in 1991. He was a student at the University of Helsinki and built Linux as a free and open-source alternative to Minix, which was an operating system used for academic purposes.
